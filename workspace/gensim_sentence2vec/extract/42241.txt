【第29回・第30回 情報統計力学研究会】
日時・場所: 平成26年7月30日 (水曜日) 午後3時00分〜 情報科学研究科棟8-11 セミナー室 
講師: Yingying Xu (東京工業大学 総合理工学研究科 樺島研究室)
題目: Bayesian signal reconstruction for 1-bit compressed sensing
講演概要: 
The 1-bit compressed sensing framework enables the recovery of a sparse
vector x from the sign information of each entry of its linear transformation. Discarding
the amplitude information can significantly reduce the amount of data, which is
highly beneficial in practical applications.  We present a Bayesian
approach to signal reconstruction for 1-bit compressed sensing, and analyze its typical
performance using statistical mechanics. Utilizing the replica method, we show that
the Bayesian approach enables better reconstruction than the L1-norm minimization
approach, asymptotically saturating the performance obtained when the non-zero
entries positions of the signal are known. We also test a message passing algorithm
for signal reconstruction on the basis of belief propagation. The results of numerical
experiments are consistent with those of the theoretical analysis.
講師: 大関真之 (京都大学 情報学研究科)
題目: 半教師ありクラスタリングの統計力学的性能評価
講演概要: 
高次元データを分類する素朴な手法として知られる教師なし学習の一例でもあるクラスタリング、
その精度を高めるためにラベルが既についたデータを利用した教師あり学習の要素を加えて、
半教師ありクラスタリングについて、その典型的性能評価を統計力学と呼ばれる物理学の処方箋に基づき行った.
その性能を実際に達成するアルゴリズムを作る事が次に要求されることであるが、
これについても近年注目を集めるメッセージ伝搬法を駆使する事で実現することがわかった.
実際に半教師ありクラスタリングを実行する方法として、未分類データのラベルを隠れ変数ととらえて、
EMアルゴリズムを適用することが考えられるが、メッセージ伝搬法による手法はEMアルゴリズムによる性能より有効であることがわかった.
【第28回 情報統計力学研究会】
日時・場所: 平成26年7月9日 (水曜日) 午後4時00分〜 情報科学研究科棟8-11 セミナー室 
講師: Haiping Huang (東京工業大学 総合理工学研究科 樺島研究室)
講演題目: Understanding low-density parity-check codes from a statistical physics perspective
講演概要: 
In this talk, I  provide a detailed quantitative  analysis of low-density parity-check (LDPC) codes, in terms of code optimization, 
frozen glassy phase and improved decoding algorithms when no prior knowledge of channel statistics is assumed, under both replica symmetric 
and first step replica symmetry breaking approximations. (1) By evaluating the entropy function at the Nishimori temperature, 
we found that irregular code constructions have superior decoding performance compared to  the regular counterparts. 
(2) For general temperatures, we show that the instability of the mean-field calculation takes place only after the entropy crisis, 
suggesting the presence of a frozen glassy phase. (3) Without prior knowledge of the channel noise, a reinforced strategy on normal belief propagation 
will boost the decoding threshold to a higher value than the belief propagation without  the reinforcement. 
The complexity function characterizing the metastable state landscape in this situation is also computed and analyzed.

【第25回 情報統計力学研究会】
日時・場所: 平成25年8月28日 (水曜日) 午後4時30分〜 情報科学研究科棟8-11 セミナー室 
講師: Anirban Chakraborti (Chair of Quantitative Finance, Ecole Centrale Paris, France)
講演題目: An Econophysicist's way of studying the dynamic evolution of the market
講演概要: 
We first present empirical studies revealing the statistical properties of financial time series, 
and the widely acknowledged stylized facts, which describe the returns of financial assets—fat tails, 
volatility clustering, autocorrelation, etc. We then discuss in a pedagogical manner, with illustrations of 
how the complex economic system (financial market) enriches the list of existing dynamical systems that 
physicists have been studying for long. Finally, we present a perspective on correlation studies in the price time-series of stocks, 
using (i) the minimum spanning tree approach and (ii) the multidimensional scaling tools. 
The first is a method for finding a hierarchical arrangement of stocks by using correlations of asset returns. 
With an appropriate metric, based on the correlation matrix, a fully connected graph is defined in which the nodes are companies, 
or stocks, and the distances between them are obtained from the corresponding correlation coefficients. 
The minimum spanning tree is generated from the graph by selecting the most important correlations and it is used to 
identify clusters of companies. The second is a set of data analysis techniques that display the structure of distance-like data 
as a geometrical picture, where again the distances between them are obtained from the corresponding correlation coefficients. 
We then use clustering algorithms to identify clusters of companies. 
The aim is to study the dynamical evolution of the correlations in the market.

重ね合わせの原理により並列型の処理を行うことの出来る量子コンピュータ.

【第18回 情報統計力学研究会】
日時・場所:  平成23年5月16日(月曜日) 午後4時半〜 情報科学研究科棟8-11 セミナー室 ※ 震災により本年11月に延期. 
講師: Bikas K. Chakrabarti (Saha Institute of Nuclear Physics, India)
講演題目: Inequality reversal: effects of the savings propensity and correlated returns
講演概要: 
In the last decade, a large body of literature has been developed to explain the universal features of inequality 
in terms of income and wealth. By now, it is established that the distributions of income and wealth in various economies 
show a number of statistical regularities. There are several models to explain such static features of inequality in an unifying framework 
and the kinetic exchange models, in particular, provide one such framework. Here we focus on the dynamic features of inequality. 
In the process of development and growth, inequality in an economy in terms of income and wealth follows a particular pattern of rising 
in the initial stage followed by an eventual fall. This inverted U-shaped curve is known as the Kuznets Curve.
We examine the possibilities of such behavior of an economy in the context of a generalized kinetic exchange model. 
It is shown that under some specific conditions, our model economy indeed shows inequality reversal.
参考文献: 
A.S. Chakrabarti and B.K. Chakrabarti, Physica A, 389 17 3572 (2010).

【第14回 情報統計力学研究会】

講演題目: 視覚的形態によるオブジェクト認知の処理過程 --- 心理物理学と認知心理学の観点から --- 
講演概要: 
心理学における視覚的オブジェクト認知の研究では,最初にオブジェクトをシーンから抽出する段階を経て,
オブジェクトを再認する段階へと移行する階層的構造が仮定されている(例えばNakayama, He, & Shimojo, 1995)。
このことから,オブジェクト認知には検出,識別,同定,カテゴリー化といった決定段階が考えられている。
これは,心理物理学と認知心理学の2つのアプローチから得られた知見であり,それぞれのアプローチがオブジェクト認知の異なった側面を示している。
心理物理学では,オブジェクトの高次の知覚表象について調べられ,認知心理学では,オブジェクトの同定やカテゴリー化,再認といって処理過程について調べられてきた。
しかしながら,これらの知見についての統合的な研究はまだ十分に行われていない。
そこで,オブジェクト認知において最も重要な視覚情報であると考えられる形態に焦点を当て,
検出,識別,同定,カテゴリー化といったそれぞれの段階でどのような形態情報との関連が見出されるかを示す。

講演概要: 
Our understanding of earthquakes is based on the theory
of plate tectonics. Earthquake dynamics is the study of the
interactions of plates (solid disjoint parts of the lithosphere)
which produce seismic activity. Over the last about fifty years many
models have come up which try to simulate seismic activity by
mimicking plate plate interactions. The validity of a given model
is subject to the compliance of the synthetic seismic activity it

statistical features of observed seismic activity. Here we present
a review of two such models of earthquake dynamics with main focus
on a relatively new model namely The Two Fractal Overlap Model.

講演題目: State-space methods for real-time cortical control ofneuroprosthetic devices
講演概要: 
A number of important data analysis problems in neuroscience can be solved using statespace
models. The optimal estimate of the state is its conditional expectation given the
observation histories, but this expectation is computationally demanding when nonlinearities
are present. Various authors have therefore used Gaussian approximations to
posterior densities that appear in the formulation. In the first part of the presentation,
we investigate this approach, showing that the errors introduced by the approximation
are not compounded across time. We then consider second-order expansions, and show
that they can provide second-order accuracy in state estimates — but that no additional
accuracy is possible by higher-order approximations. We discuss implementation of these
methods and illustrate by decoding multielectrode motor cortical data.
In the second part of the presentation, we have developed real-time software which
implements our decoding methods in conjunction with a brain-machine interface. We
show results from an experiment in which a monkey controlled a computer cursor using
neural signals processed with these methods.

講演題目: A Dynamical Structure of High Frequency Data for Yen Dollar Exchange Rate
講演概要: 
With the development of computer technology, it is now possible to follow the price change in real-time.
Such the novelty of high frequency data has revealed the distinct properties, which can not be explained
by low frequency data analysis so far. We also here present another evidence of the unique property of
high frequency data.
The purpose of this paper is to extract a common dynamical structure from the high frequency yendollar
exchange rates, which has been traditionally regarded as one of the most unbiased and uncorrelated
market time series. We analyze tick-by-tick data, most high frequency data available, of yen-dollar
currency exchange rates focusing on two aspects.
The first aspect is up down directional movement of prices. We show that a dynamical structure with
non-linear trend can be observed in binarized data presenting the direction of up and down movement
of prices, although it is not apparently seen from the price change itself. We also find that there exists a
nontrivial structure in conditional probability of the high frequency binary data. The structure can be
well captured by an extended non-linear logit model referring up to previous five states. This empirical
result is consistent with dealers perceptions, which slowly changes with a time scale of few minutes.
The other one is the state in which the price stays the same rate. We find that there is a rhythmic
behavior in this state, which tends to take consecutive states for some fixed times. This characteristic
feature is not observed in binary up down data. The model with noise and delay inspired from research
of stochastic resonance can reproduce the rhythmic behavior well.
It should be noted that such structure is one of the most vivid example of the high frequency data
property, which has not been reported in the studies of low frequency data such as of the order of hoursor days.

講演題目: Oscillatory Phenomena in Neural Network Model of Hippocampal CA3 Region
講演概要: 
Theta and gamma oscillations observed in the hippocampus are considered to be associated
with the memory function in the brain. However a mechanism of generation of the theta and the
gamma oscillation is not clarified yet. In order to reveal the oscillatory phenomena in the hippocampus,
we propose a hippocampal CA3 model with excitatory neurons and inhibitory neurons using Hodgkin-
Huxley equations and investigate the oscillatory phenomena in the model [1]. Our model consists of
two layers; one is a layer of excitatory neurons and the other a layer of inhibitory neurons. We assume
inter-layer synaptic connections and also intra-layer synaptic connections. The results by numerical
simulations show the theta oscillation or the gamma oscillation occurs on both layers for certain values
of the synaptic strengths. We also find that the theta oscillation occurs on a layer while the gamma oscillation
occurs on the other layer for other values of the synaptic strengths. We reveal that the synaptic
strengths are an important factor for the generation of the theta and the gamma oscillation.
